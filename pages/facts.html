Russian Napoleon Cake

In 1912, the first iteration of the cake was crafted to honor the 100th anniversary of the country's victory over Napoleon and his troops. Initially, the cake was much smaller:
a single-serve, cream-filled pastry crafted to resemble the defeated Frenchman's triangular bicorne.

Moon Cake

One of the most beloved traditional pastries in East and Southeast Asia, mooncakes are synonymous with the Chinese Mid-Autumn Festival, a holiday that falls on the full moon, i.e., the fifteenth day of the eighth lunar month. (In the Gregorian calendar, the date is typically in late September or early October.)

Tembleque

The name tembleque means wiggly, referring to a slight wiggle when the pudding is shaken. This rich dessert can also be flavored with vanilla, nutmeg, or rum, if desired. It is especially popular at birthday parties and similar festive occasions.
